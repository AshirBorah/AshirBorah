### About

I'm a PhD candidate in the Biological & Medical Informatics program at UCSF, jointly mentored by [Luke Gilbert](https://www.gilbertlabucsf.com/) and [Brian Hie](https://www.brianhie.com/) at the Arc Institute. My research sits at the intersection of **functional genomics, virology, and computational biology** — combining CRISPR-based screening, assay development, and high-dimensional data analysis to study gene regulation, host–virus interactions, and disease mechanisms.

In parallel, I build **AI-enabled research systems** — LLM councils, research agents, APIs, and multi-agent workflows for literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, and experimental planning.

Before UCSF, I spent nearly three years at the Broad Institute on the [Cancer Dependency Map](https://depmap.org), applying statistical modeling and machine learning to genome-scale perturbation data to discover and validate cancer targets.

### 🔬 What I work on

- **CRISPR functional genomics** — genome-scale knockout, interference, and Perturb-seq screens
- **Virology & host–virus biology** — assay platforms and perturbation systems for infection biology
- **AI-enabled research systems** — LLM councils, research agents, and multi-agent workflows
- **Computational ↔ experimental loops** — closing the gap between in-silico predictions and bench validation

### 🌱 Currently

- Running screens to discover protein function
- Reading to build new biological systems
